Morning Briefing

Get set up in about 10 minutes. You'll need to create two free accounts to get your API keys.

Step 1 — Get your Anthropic API key

Anthropic's AI writes your briefing script each morning.

  1. Go to console.anthropic.com and create a free account.
  2. Once logged in, click API Keys in the left sidebar.
  3. Click Create Key, give it any name (e.g. "Morning Briefing"), and copy the key — it starts with sk-ant-.
  4. Save it somewhere safe. You won't be able to see it again.

Cost: Anthropic charges per use. A single daily briefing costs roughly $0.01–0.03. You'll need to add a credit card and load a small amount of credit (e.g. $5 lasts months).

Step 2 — Get your ElevenLabs API key and Voice ID

ElevenLabs converts the script into a natural-sounding voice.

  1. Go to elevenlabs.io and create a free account.
  2. Once logged in, click your profile icon (bottom left) → Profile + API Key.
  3. Copy your API Key.
  4. Now go to elevenlabs.io/app/voice-library to pick a voice you like — click Add to My Voices on any voice.
  5. Go to My Voices, click the voice you added, and copy the Voice ID shown at the bottom.

Cost: ElevenLabs free tier includes 10,000 characters/month — enough for about 10 briefings. Their $5/month plan covers a full month of daily use.

Step 3 — Create your account

Head to the sign up page and fill in:

  • Your name — the briefing will greet you by name each morning.
  • Your email — used only to send you a login link.
  • Both API keys from the steps above.
  • Your ElevenLabs Voice ID.
  • The time you want your briefing ready by and your timezone.

After signing up, check your email and click the link to activate your account.

Step 4 — Set up your alarm (iPhone)

Your account page shows a personal audio URL. Here's how to use it as your alarm:

  1. On your iPhone, open the Shortcuts app.
  2. Tap + to create a new shortcut.
  3. Tap Add Action → search for Get Contents of URL → add it.
  4. Paste your personal audio URL into the URL field.
  5. Add another action: search for Play Sound → connect it to the output of the previous step.
    (Alternatively use Encode Media → Play Sound if Play Sound doesn't work directly.)
  6. Save the shortcut with a name like "Morning Briefing".
  7. Open the Clock app → set a new alarm → tap Sound → scroll down to Shortcuts → select "Morning Briefing".

Step 4 — Set up your alarm (Android)

  1. Install Sleep as Android or any alarm app that supports URL audio streams.
  2. When creating an alarm, choose Spotify / URL as the ringtone and paste your personal audio URL.

That's it

Every morning at your chosen time the server generates a fresh briefing and your alarm plays it. Once a week it'll surprise you with something completely random instead of the news — just for fun.
